Lesbian
A woman who is romantically and/or sexually attracted to other women
Bisexual
A person who is romantically and/or sexually attracted to two genders
Attracted to men and women, women and nonbinary people, or men and nonbinary people
Nonbinary
A person who doesn’t feel comfortable identifying within the binary of man and woman
Usually a nonbinary person will use gender neutral pronouns, such as they/them or any neopronouns (pronouns that are new, such as ey, em, eirs), but not everyone chooses to use gender neutral pronouns and may instead stick to he/him, she/her, or even use multiple sets of pronouns (such as he/she/they, he/they, she/they, or she/he)
Asexual
A person who doesn’t experience sexual attraction, but may still experience romantic attraction
This can be an umbrella term for some labels such as greysexual (people who experience sexual attraction rarely) or demisexual (sexual attraction may form after an emotional connection is created
Queer
An umbrella term for anyone who isn’t cisgender or heterosexual
Not everyone uses this term, and it isn’t a term to describe everyone in the community
This is a reclaimed slur against LGBTQ+ people for being “strange, odd, or not natural”
Gay
A man who is romantically and/or sexually attracted to other men
Transgender
A person who feels more comfortable and identifies as a gender that isn’t the same as their assigned gender at birth
AMAB (assigned male at birth) and AFAB (assigned female at birth)
Usually FTM (female to male, or a transman) and MTF (male to female, or a transwoman), but some nonbinary people also identify as trans (not all do)
A person who does identify with their assigned gender at birth is cisgender
Intersex
A person who is born with sex characteristics that aren’t typical of binary sex characteristics
Used to describe a wide range of natural body variations, such as chromosomes, genitals, or gonads.
Aromantic
A person who doesn’t experience romantic attraction, but may still experience sexual attraction
This can be an umbrella term for some labels such as grey-aromantic (people who experience romantic attraction rarely) or demiromantic (romantic attraction may form after an emotional connection is created)
Pansexual
A person who is attracted to two or more genders, usually attracted to people and personalities
